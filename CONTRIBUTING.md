# Contributing to Designer's Epistemology

This framework is a living document. It was built from one person's experience on one enzyme system in one lab. Its value grows every time someone runs it on a different system, with different mentees, and documents what broke.

If you are reading this as a Siegel Lab student, a D2D (Design-to-Data) network member, or anyone doing real design work with real mentees — your experience is what this document needs next.

---

## What Kinds of Contributions Matter

### Worked Examples from Other Enzyme Systems

The BglB example is a starting point, not a boundary. If you have run a version of this framework — formally or informally — on a different enzyme system, the most valuable thing you can contribute is your own theory injection map and a brief description of where the framework held and where it needed adaptation.

A worked example should include:

- The enzyme system and the design question you were working on
- A theory injection map for that system (see `/examples/bglb/` for format)
- A short reflection: which movements worked as described, which needed modification, and what you would tell the next person who runs this on the same system

It does not need to be polished. Honest and specific is more useful than clean and generic.

---

### Refinements to the Core Framework

The six movements and the core principles are v0.1. If you have run this and found that a movement doesn't work as described — that the boundary between two movements is wrong, that a principle needs qualification, that something important is missing — open an issue and describe what you observed.

What makes a useful refinement contribution:

- A specific observation, not a general opinion. *Movement II breaks down when the student has no prior exposure to primary literature* is useful. *The framework needs more structure* is not.
- A proposed change with reasoning. What would you change, and why do you think it would work better?
- Acknowledgment of tradeoffs. Every change to a framework involves giving something up. What does your proposed change sacrifice?

---

### Reasoning Documents and Student Artifacts

If you have gone through this framework as a mentee and are willing to share your reasoning documents — Movement IV design commitments, troubleshooting notes, Movement VI narratives — these are enormously useful as concrete reference material for future students.

They should be contributed to a system-specific `/examples/` subfolder. They can be anonymized. Partial documents are fine. An honest failed attempt is more instructive than a polished success.

---

### Corrections and Clarifications

If something in the framework is unclear, contradictory, or wrong — open an issue. Describe specifically what confused you or what you think is incorrect. This includes the theory injection map: if a resource link is broken, a directed question doesn't land, or a better resource exists, say so.

---

## What Does Not Belong Here

This repo is not a general repository for project-based learning resources, enzyme design protocols, or lab onboarding documentation. Contributions should connect directly to the mentorship framework — to how students develop the designer's posture, how mentors calibrate friction, or how theory gets delivered at the right moment.

If you are unsure whether something belongs, open an issue and ask before writing the contribution.

---

## How to Contribute

1. **Fork the repository.**

2. **Create a branch** with a descriptive name — `add-lipase-example`, `revise-movement-ii`, `fix-resource-links`.

3. **Make your changes.** Follow the existing document structure and tone. Write in plain language. Be specific. Be honest about uncertainty.

4. **Open a pull request** with a short description of what you changed and why. If it is a new worked example, say which enzyme system and roughly how many mentees you ran the framework with.

5. **Expect dialogue.** Contributions may be discussed before merging. This is not gatekeeping — it is the same posture the framework asks of mentees. Reasoning should be visible and open to interrogation.

---

## On Tone

The framework has a specific voice — direct, honest about uncertainty, resistant to generic advice. Contributions should maintain that voice. If you find yourself writing something that could appear in any educational framework document without modification, it probably needs to be made more specific.

The goal is a document that carries real thinking from real people who did real work. Generic is the thing this framework is explicitly built against.

---

## A Note on Credit

Contributors will be acknowledged in the repository. If your worked example or refinement substantially shapes the framework, that will be reflected in the document history and acknowledgments section.

This is not a document that belongs to one person. It belongs to the lab culture and network that uses and improves it. Contribute accordingly.

---

*If you ran this framework and something broke in a way not described here — that is the most important thing you can tell us. Open an issue. Write it down. The next person needs to know.*
